About Me

My journey in the realm of thermofluids has been nothing short of exhilarating. From delving into the complexities of heat transfer to navigating the fluid dynamics maze, I've developed a keen appreciation for the nuanced interplay of energy and matter. It's like conducting a symphony where the notes are replaced by temperatures, pressures, and velocities.

As the Chassis Lead, I'm the backbone of our team, ensuring that the mechanical heart of our electric race car beats efficiently and effectively. Whether it's optimizing the chassis for aerodynamics, ensuring the structural rigidity of the chassis, or diving into the nitty-gritty of mechanical systems, I thrive on the challenge of creating a vehicle that seamlessly fuses form and function. What gets my gears turning is not just the theoretical understanding but the practical application of my knowledge. I revel in the hands-on experience of bringing concepts to life, be it in the workshop, on the track, or in the midst of a design brainstorm. There's something magical about seeing equations transform into a roaring machine. Beyond the technical aspects, I'm a firm believer in collaboration and effective communication. Being part of a Formula Student team isn't just about individual brilliance; it's about the synergy of minds working towards a common goal. My role extends beyond the nuts and bolts, reaching into the realm of leadership and teamwork.

So, whether it's deciphering the secrets of thermofluids, fine-tuning the intricacies of mechanical design, or steering the course as Chassis Lead, I'm here to embrace the challenges, learn from the journey, and drive towards engineering excellence.

MAC EV4 Chassis

What?

  • As part of the 2023 FSAE competition, designed and built a fully electric 1/4-scale formula style race car
  • Designed all chassis components such as the monocoque, roll hoops, and jackbar
  • For the monocoque, designed the ideal layup plan to minimize cost and weight
  • For the roll hoops, designed the ideal roll cage that would protect the driver in case of roll over, which included:
    • designing the geometry that would match our old a-surface
    • designing mounts that would meet the new FSAE rules and ensure the roll hoops were well secured
  • For the jackbar, improved the previous year's design by reducing its weight
  • When designing, the Formula SAE Electric Rules had to be followed

How?

  • Used SolidWorks to design multiple components of the chassis
  • Performed thorough research to optimized all designs
  • Performed FEA using Ansys Workbench and SolidWorks to validate all designs
  • Created GD&T drawings of all parts
  • Used MS Excel to calculate the ideal layup plan that would pass the rules
  • Assisted all steps of the monocoque manufacturing
  • Cut the carbon fiber and foam core and laying them on the mold
  • Performed vacuum infusion
  • Tested carbon fiber panels using a three point bend test and perimeter shear test
  • Water jet multiple components of the chassis
  • Machined several components using a Lathe, Drill Press, and Mill

Results

  • Reduced manufacturing time by 25% by doing the following:
    • Simplifying the geometry of the roll hoops
    • Improving the vacuum infusion process
  • Improved hardpoints’ stiffness by 200% by using balsa wood as the core material
  • Provided enough torsional stiffness for a smooth handling of the vehicle
  • All chassis components adhered to all the rules and fulfilled its purpose

MAC EV5 Carbon Moncocoque

What?

  • Designed and manufactured a carbon monocoque type chassis for an FSAE electric car
  • Created a layup plan that minimizes weight and cost while maximizing torsional stiffness
  • When designing, the Formula SAE Electric Rules had to be followed

How?

  • Used SolidWorks to design a CAD model of the monocoque
  • Performed FEA using Ansys Workbench to determine whether the model provided the desired torsional stiffness
  • Used MS Excel to calculate a layup plan that passes the rules while minimizing the cost

Results

  • Reduced monocoque weight by 75%
  • Reduced material cost by 50%
  • Improved design simplicity reducing manufacturing time by at least 50%
  • Improved vacuum infusion process by reducing sharp corner radii and simplifying the geometry

MAC EV5 Steel Spaceframe

What?

  • Designed a steel tube spaceframe chassis for an FSAE electric car
  • Minimized weight of the chassis
  • Simplified the design for manufacturing reasons
  • Used model to compare between monocoque, spaceframe, and hybrid options for the chassis type
  • When designing, the Formula SAE Electric Rules had to be followed

How?

  • Used SolidWorks to design a CAD model of the spaceframe
  • Performed simulations using FEA and Ansys to determine the ideal geometry
  • Researched FSAE spaceframe designs since the team had never done one and studied load path analysis to optimize the geometry

Results

  • Design was capable of with 10 kN of force from each wheel
  • Provided enough torsional stiffness for a smooth handling of the vehicle
  • Capable of protecting the driver in case of front, side, and rear impact and roll over
  • Weighted less 25% than the average FSAE steel spaceframe
